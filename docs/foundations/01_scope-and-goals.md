# 01 Scope and Goals

## Objective

Build an agent-based simulation framework that can test whether macro outcomes (overshoot, inequality, authoritarian drift, growth dependence) can emerge from explicit micro-to-meso rules.

## Primary Research Question

Can a system with bounded biophysical resources, human status/surplus drives, and institutional adaptation produce dynamics that resemble real-world trajectories?

## Non-Goals (Phase 1)

- Perfect prediction of real history.
- One “true” model.
- Full global digital twin.

## Model Design Constraints

- Every assumption must be configurable and documented.
- Components must be replaceable (behavioral rules, market clearing, policy modules).
- Outcomes must be analyzable at agent and system levels.

## Core Output Types

- Time-series: energy throughput, inequality, emissions, trust, rights provisioning.
- Distribution snapshots: wealth/resource/status distributions.
- Regime diagnostics: growth, stagnation, collapse, coercive stabilization.
- Counterfactual comparisons across assumption sets.
