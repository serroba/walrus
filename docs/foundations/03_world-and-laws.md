# 03 World Model and Laws

## Entities

1. Agents (households/workers/firms/elites/state actors).
2. Institutions (market rules, state controls, rights regimes, media systems).
3. Resource systems (energy, materials, land, ecosystem services).
4. Infrastructure (grids, logistics, extraction/refining capacity).
5. Settlements/polities (group units with explicit population scale and subsistence mode).

## Minimal Law Set (v0)

1. Conservation-like accounting:
   - Material and energy balances tracked explicitly.
2. Bounded extraction:
   - Access depends on technology, capital, and remaining stock quality.
3. Regeneration and degradation:
   - Renewable flows regenerate; overshoot degrades future carrying capacity.
4. Surplus allocation:
   - Surplus split among consumption, reinvestment, coercion, and restoration.
5. Institutional adaptation:
   - Rules update with lag based on stress, legitimacy, and coalition power.
6. Behavioral heterogeneity:
   - Agents vary in preferences, risk tolerance, and coordination capacity.
7. Group scale transition:
   - As `N` grows, social coordination changes from kin-enforced norms toward formal institutions.
8. Subsistence regime transition:
   - Regime shifts (hunter-gatherer -> sedentary -> agricultural) alter surplus, storage, hierarchy, and conflict dynamics.

## Stress Channels

- Resource stress -> price stress -> political stress -> institutional response.
- Information stress (misinformation/censorship) -> trust shifts -> coordination quality.
- Climate/ecological shocks -> productivity and migration pressure.

## Scenario Knobs

- Fossil abundance and decline profile.
- Renewable build constraints.
- Redistribution intensity.
- Authoritarian response threshold.
- Trade/globalization coupling strength.
- Group-size transition thresholds.
- Regime-switch triggers (climate, technology, storage productivity).
